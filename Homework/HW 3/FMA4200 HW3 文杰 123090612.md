## (a) Is the model stationary?

**Yes.**
A moving Average ($MA$) model of any order $q$ is **always stationary**.

## (b) Write down the invertibility condition of the model.

For an $MA(q)$ model to be invertible, the **roots of its characteristic polynomial** must lie outside the unit circle.

For this specific $MA(2)$ process, the characteristic equation is defined as:

$$1 + a_1 z + a_2 z^2 = 0$$

The invertibility condition requires that for all complex roots $z$ that satisfy this equation, their magnitude must be greater than one:

$$|z| > 1$$

This ensures that the $MA$ process can be uniquely represented as an $AR(\infty)$ process.

## (c) Find the mean, variance, autocovariance function, and autocorrelation function.

Using the general formulas provided for $MA(q)$ models where $q=2$:

### 1. Mean

The expectation of the process is simply the constant term:

$$\mathbb{E}[X_t] = \mu \text{}$$

### 2. Variance

The variance (or the autocovariance at lag 0, $\gamma_0$) is:

$$\text{Var}(X_t) = \gamma(0) = \sigma^2(1 + a_1^2 + a_2^2) \text{}$$

### 3. ACVF $\gamma(k) = \text{Cov}(X_t, X_{t-k})$

- **Lag 1:** $\gamma(1) = \sigma^2(a_1 + a_1 a_2)$
    
- **Lag 2:** $\gamma(2) = \sigma^2 a_2$
    
- **Lag $k > 2$:** $\gamma(k) = 0$ (The ACVF "cuts off" after lag $q$)
    

### 4. ACF $\rho(k) = \frac{\gamma(k)}{\gamma(0)}$

- **Lag 1:** $\rho(1) = \frac{a_1 + a_1 a_2}{1 + a_1^2 + a_2^2}$
    
- **Lag 2:** $\rho(2) = \frac{a_2}{1 + a_1^2 + a_2^2}$
    
- **Lag $k > 2$:** $\rho(k) = 0$

# 2

## (a) Is the model invertible?

**Yes.**
AR models are always invertible.

## (b) Stationary Condition of the AR(2) Process

For an AR(2) model to be stationary, the roots of its **characteristic polynomial** must lie outside the unit circle. Specifically:

The characteristic equation is:

$$1 - b_1 z - b_2 z^2 = 0$$

The **stationary condition** is that both roots $z_1, z_2$ of this equation must satisfy $|z_i| > 1$ (their magnitudes must be greater than 1).

Explicitly, in terms of the coefficients $b_1$ and $b_2$, this corresponds to the following three inequalities:

1. $b_1 + b_2 < 1$
    
2. $b_2 - b_1 < 1$
    
3. $|b_2| < 1$
    

## (c) Moments and Correlation Functions

If the AR(2) process is stationary, its statistical properties are derived as follows:

- **Mean ($\mathbb{E}[X_t]$):**
    
    The mean $\mu$ is given by:
    
    $$\mathbb{E}[X_t] = \mu = \frac{c}{1 - b_1 - b_2}$$
    
- **ACVF, $\gamma(k)$:**
    
    The ACVF satisfies the **Yule-Walker equations**:
    
    $$\gamma(k) = b_1 \gamma(k-1) + b_2 \gamma(k-2), \quad k \geq 1$$
    
    To find the specific values, solve the system for $k=0, 1, 2$:
    
    - $\gamma(0) = b_1\gamma(1) + b_2\gamma(2) + \sigma^2$
        
    - $\gamma(1) = b_1\gamma(0) + b_2\gamma(1)$
        
    - $\gamma(2) = b_1\gamma(1) + b_2\gamma(0)$
        
- **Variance ($\text{Var}(X_t) = \gamma(0)$):**
    
    By solving the system above, the variance is:
    
    $$\gamma(0) = \left( \frac{1 - b_2}{1 + b_2} \right) \frac{\sigma^2}{(1 - b_2)^2 - b_1^2}$$
    
- **ACF, $\rho(k) = \gamma(k)/\gamma(0)$:**
    
    For $k=1$:
    
    $$\rho(1) = \frac{b_1}{1 - b_2}$$
    
    For $k \geq 2$, it follows the same recursive relationship as the ACVF:
    
    $$\rho(k) = b_1 \rho(k-1) + b_2 \rho(k-2)$$
    
    The ACF of a stationary AR process typically exhibits **exponential decay** and may involve damped oscillations depending on the coefficients.
